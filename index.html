FIELD MANUAL — PID IN SIXTY SECONDS

The stabilizer measures the gap between commanded and actual gun azimuth (the error) and outputs drive torque: u = Kp·e + Ki·∫e dt − Kd·ω

Proportional (Kp) pushes harder the further you are from the aim point. It sets how fast the gun gets moving — but a spring alone always overshoots.

Derivative (Kd) resists turret rate, like a damper. It turns ringing into a clean, critically damped arrival. Underdamped = overshoots and misses; overdamped = tracks too slowly to follow the wargame.

Integral (Ki) accumulates leftover error over time. It is the only term that can null a sustained bias such as terrain torque on the trunnion — but wind it up too far and the overshoot comes back.

Rule of thumb for this bench: damping ratio ≈ (Kd + b) / (2·√(Kp·J)). Aim near 1.0 for critical damping, then add just enough Ki to hold zero under disturbance. Watch the drive-effort readout — a saturated drive behaves worse than the math says.
